The American Buffalo is a symbol of abundance and manifestation. The lesson learned by the Lakota is one does not have to struggle to survive if the right action is joined by the right prayer. By learning to unite the mundane with the divine, all that will be needed will be provided.



Abundance for generations to come.

We have enough resources available to meet and improve our standard of living for generations to come.



Instead, the world economic-engine is based upon consuming and throwing away everything we use. Yet inside everything we throw away are lego blocks that could be used to build something else.



This is a movement.

The Native Americans are well known for using every part of a Buffalo. Its stomach, for example, became a soup kettle and its horns the spoons to eat the soup. It provided them all they needed, and as a result, it has become our symbol of abundance.



The Buffalo Pledge
  1. Planned Obsolescence is not a long-term sustainable model for economic prosperity.
  2. We should never throw away valuable resources that can be repurposed into new utility
  3. The Circular economy should be the first supplier of all resources, with extraction as a last resort.

The Buffalo Network is a community of individuals and organizations that believe that we can return to the Native American principle of abundance. This is possible through merging technology with a mind shift in how we approach value. Our perception must shift from planned obsolescence towards circular economies. Technology offers opportunities to catalyze such a shift such as through tracking and incentivizing responsible circular consumption.

Building tools for the movement.

This April 12-14 2019, team Buffalo (Poseiden) will be building the buffalo.network protocol at the Odyssey Hackathon in Groningen, Netherlands. The goal is to materalize this cultural shift with the necessary technology.

This will be a web app where anyone can register their phone for it's rare metals to be recycled back into the commons. Giving to the commons means you can pull from the commons.

The core components of this blockchain app will be to:

Track

Finite metals, like colbalt, are sitting in drawers as unused old phones across the world. Before we can circulate these metals back into production, we need to begin tracking where it's sitting around.

Incentivize

Old phones aren't that valuable, even if the resources inside are. Thus, we'll be implementing non-monetary incentives that gamifies participation. Votes, credits, access for later... join the discussion!

Distribute

When enough resources are ready to go, an organization makes a proposal for how they will use it. Then individuals who can vote and send their devices via the mail (for now, drones later). Participation is rewarded.
